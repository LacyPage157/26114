Who is our founder?

Christopher High Robotics was pitched and founded by Frank English, where he initially struggled to obtain funding and approval. After mustering the initiative to pitch the idea to his school, Frank English was able to single handedly obtain approval and a budget from the school.

Who else founded 26114?

Niah Fellows-Martin was the Vice President of the club, helping Frank in the intitial construction of the club. Beyond that, she was also the Engineering department head, teaching everyone how to build and design the robot. Trevor Stratton was another founding member, the head of the Manufacturing department. He 3D-printed and designed countless parts for the first robot, and taught others his ways too.

The club’s goal is to positively influence the student population
of our school and inspire them to consider a STEM career.

To this end, we have prepared
our students with a number of career ready
skills, including the operation
of tools, and an insight
into the design process.

Meet the other Officers!

We’ve had to face several challenges and we persevered through them all. Elijah, one of our secretaries, was one of the most inspirational members we had, always keeping the team good-spirited and readily engineering solutions for our robot. Daniel, our Software department head, directed the inclusion of automated features for our robot, teaching everyone else the math required to perform the advanced calculations required. Alessandra, another one of our secretaries, beyond keeping notes and keeping things running, was able to deliver a contribution to the collective idea of a perimeter system. Finally, our treasurer, Alex, has been searching for grants and funding our future!
